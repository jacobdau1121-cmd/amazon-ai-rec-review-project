Our project idea is very simple and easy to understand. When we shop online on Amazon, Taobao or JD.com, the products we see are not randomly arranged. They are all selected and recommended by AI algorithms. The AI system will give more display chances to products with good reviews and high popularity. This directly affects product sales and public popularity.
However, most people only shop online without thinking about these hidden rules. We want to use real data to answer several simple questions. （换）What kind of products do AI algorithms prefer to recommend? Are highly-rated and expensive products more likely to be recommended? Do higher-priced products always have better user reviews? In this project, we use real e-commerce data and Python tools to turn these daily questions into clear charts and reliable conclusions, so as to quantify the influence of AI recommendation systems.
Now I will explain the core logic of our project.（换）
We use a public electronic product review dataset from Amazon. It includes real product prices, user ratings and review likes. But this dataset does not have a direct label to show whether a product is recommended by AI or not. Based on common e-commerce rules, we make a reasonable and scientific judgment: AI recommendation systems always prefer to push products with high popularity, good reputation and high user interaction.
So we divide all products into two groups. The first group includes popular products with high scores and more interactions, which are more likely to get AI recommendation and online exposure. The second group includes ordinary products with low scores and few interactions, which rarely get AI recommendation. By comparing these two groups, we can clearly analyze the preference and real influence of e-commerce AI recommendation systems. This is the core logic of our whole project.
